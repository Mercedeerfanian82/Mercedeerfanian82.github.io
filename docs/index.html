Misophonia Soundscape Pupillometry Decision-Making EEG · fMRI Psychophysiology Affective Neuroscience & Psychopathology View Publications ORCID Profile Get in Touch ABOUT Who I Am I am a researcher at ESSCA, France, with a PhD in Cognitive Neuroscience from University College London (UCL), funded by the European Research Council (ERC). My academic journey spans clinical psychology, affective neuroscience (MRes, joint degree from Maastricht University and the Medical University of Florence), and cognitive neuroscience. My research integrates psychophysiological measures — including pupillometry, electrodermal activity, EEG, and fMRI — with advanced behavioural paradigms to understand how humans perceive, process, and respond to their auditory and social environments. I am particularly known for my contributions to misophonia research, soundscape science, and the psychophysiology of auditory perception. I am also passionate about teaching quantitative methods, statistics, and experimental psychology across psychology, economics, and business education contexts. Beyond the lab, I create science podcasts for the public, play professional badminton, and am a frequent voice in international media on topics of sound and the mind. 35+ Publications 10+ Conference Presentations 12+ Grants & Awards 20+ Media Appearances RESEARCH Research Interests 🔊 Misophonia Investigating the neurobiological, psychophysiological, and cognitive underpinnings of misophonia — including its relationship with emotional regulation, empathy, and sensory processing disorders. 🌆 Soundscape Science Large-scale studies on how urban acoustic environments affect psychological well-being, physiological arousal, and subjective perception — contributing to international soundscape indices (SSID). 👁️ Pupillometry & Psychophysiology Using pupil-linked arousal, electrodermal activity, ECG, and EEG as objective, continuous indices of cognitive and affective processes in auditory and decision-making paradigms. 🧠 Decision-Making Examining how cognitive load, default nudges, and affective states shape human decision-making, using behavioural and occulomotor measures (eye-tracking, pupillometry). 📊 Quantitative Methods Developing and applying advanced statistical and computational approaches — including confirmatory factor analysis, IRT, and psychometric evaluation — to psychological and neuroscientific data. 🎓 Teaching & Pedagogy Teaching quantitative research methods, statistics, experimental psychology, and behavioural science across psychology, economics, and business education at undergraduate and postgraduate levels.
